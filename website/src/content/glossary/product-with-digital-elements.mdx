A product with digital elements is any software or hardware product and its
remote data processing solutions, including software or hardware components
placed on the market separately, as defined in [Article 3(1)][art-3] of the
Cyber Resilience Act. The definition is the entry point to the entire regulation:
everything the CRA requires attaches to products that meet it and are made
available on the EU market in the course of a commercial activity.

Software qualifies on its own, without any hardware: self-hosted server
applications, on-prem agents, installers, desktop clients, mobile apps, and
standalone components such as libraries are all products with digital elements
when supplied commercially. Pure cloud services are not covered, unless they
qualify as a remote data processing solution belonging to a product.
Non-commercial open-source software is also out of scope.

For products meeting the definition, the CRA's essential requirements,
support-period obligations, and, from December 11, 2027, conformity assessment
and CE marking apply. Start with
[does the CRA apply to your company](/cyber-resilience-act/does-the-cra-apply/) to classify
your own products.
